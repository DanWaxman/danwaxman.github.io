Calculus of Variations

The calculus of variations is a field where we look at small changes in the arguments of functionals. These quick notes were prepared for a QUEST, a collection of 15 minute talks for the Stony Brook Math Club.

To get through interesting results within 15 minutes, these notes quickly define some relevant terms and give moral results on vartions, namely the Euler-Lagrange equations. Then, they finish up by outlining and solving the Brachistochrone problem.

You can find the slides I used for this talk here. Eventually, math club will post videos of the talks. When they do, I'll put that link here as well.

Thermal Physics

These aren't done yet, but the page seemed lonely. I'm just rephrasing some results of statistical mechanics, basing off of Kittel & Kroemer and Landau & Lifshitz.

I found that Kittel & Kroemer is afraid of setting up mathematical formalism for everything, and Landau & Lifshitz is (as always) super intimidating, but amazing. So this is a sort of combination of the two, trying to get the mathematical clarity and rigor of Landau & Lifshitz with the accessibility of Kittel & Kroemer.

I want to finish these before the end of August. Hopefully it'll be useful to someone in the future.
